Story Requirements

- Target age: 3–6 years
- Reading time: approximately 2 minutes
- Exactly 6 story slides
- Gentle pacing
- Positive ending
- Educational but entertaining
- No violence
- No scary scenes
- No inappropriate content
- Each slide should contain 35–50 words
- Every slide should naturally continue from the previous one
- Stories may take place during any time of day.
- The final scene should end peacefully with a warm emotional conclusion.
- The ending should help children feel safe, happy and calm before bedtime if parents choose to read it then.

==================================================
LANGUAGE LEVEL (VERY IMPORTANT)
==================================================

Write for children aged 3–6 years.

Every sentence must be easy enough for a 5-year-old child in India to understand after hearing it once.

Use only everyday words.

Maximum sentence length:
8 words whenever possible.
Never more than 12 words.

Use short paragraphs.

Use active voice.

Avoid poetic language.

Avoid descriptive writing that slows the story.

Avoid difficult vocabulary.

Never use words like:

wondered
marvelled
gazed
extraordinary
magnificent
splendid
delighted
astonished
graceful
enchanted
bewildered
remarkable
sparkling
shimmering
glowing softly
nestled
wandered
peacefully drifted

Instead use words like:

big
small
happy
sad
run
jump
look
play
help
share
friend
home
tree
bird
flower
smile
thank you
please

Dialogue should sound like real children talking.

Example:

"Come and play!"

instead of

"Would you care to accompany me?"

Every slide should feel like a parent is reading aloud.

Before returning the JSON:

Read every sentence again.

If any sentence sounds difficult for a 5-year-old child, rewrite it using easier words.

Do not return the story until every page passes this check.

Keep the main character's appearance and personality consistent throughout the story.
