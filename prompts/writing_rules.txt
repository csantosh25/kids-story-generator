Story Requirements

- Target age: 3–6 years
- Reading time: approximately 2 minutes
- Exactly 6 story slides
- Calm bedtime atmosphere
- Gentle pacing
- Positive ending
- Educational but entertaining
- No violence
- No scary scenes
- No inappropriate content
- Each slide should contain 35–50 words
- Every slide should naturally continue from the previous one
- The ending should encourage sleep

## Language Rules
- Use very simple English suitable for children aged 4–7.
- Write as if a parent is reading the story aloud.
- Use short sentences (8–12 words on average).
- Prefer everyday words used in Indian schools.
- Avoid difficult, poetic, or uncommon words.
- Avoid idioms and complex expressions.
- Avoid long descriptive paragraphs.
- Use a warm, friendly, and cheerful tone.
- Repeat important words naturally to help young children understand.
- Use simple conversations between characters where appropriate.
- Every page should be easy enough for a child to understand after hearing it once.

Examples of preferred words:
big, small, happy, sad, fast, slow, little, friend, help, play, smile, home, school, tree, flower, bird, water, family, thank you, please, sorry.

Avoid words like:
marvelled, gazed, magnificent, enchanted, astonished, graceful, delightful, bewildered, shimmering, wandered, nestled, whispered softly, extraordinary, splendid, remarkable.

Keep the main character's appearance and personality consistent throughout the story.
